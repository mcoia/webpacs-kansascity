General Information

What is WILO?

WILO, also known as Western InterLibrary Organization, is a group or "cluster" of Missouri libraries within the MOBIUS library consortium (see below). The WILO Library Catalog is an on-line public access catalog, containing the collections of the following western Missouri libraries: Avila University, William Jewell College, the Metropolitan Community Colleges (Blue River, Longview, Maple Woods and Penn Valley, Kansas City Art Institute, Midwestern Baptist Theological Seminary, Rockhurst University, and Saint Paul School of Theology.

[back to top]

What is MOBIUS?

MOBIUS, also known as Missouri Bibliographic Information User System, is a consortium of academic libraries with over fifty member colleges and universities in the state of Missouri. The largest single project for which MOBIUS is responsible is The Common Library Platform project. The CLP creates a virtual collection of the approximately 14 million items in the libraries of Missouri's colleges and universities and creates a single user interface that allows faculty and students to request library materials using any personal computer in any location with access to the Internet. Requested materials are delivered within one or two days of being requested by the MOBIUS Delivery System. [more]

Requesting Materials
  • Locate item.
    Tip: Check your institution's catalog. If not found, check the WILO catalog. If still not found, check the MOBIUS catalog. Only items NOT available at your institution may be requested.

  • Check status.

    AvailableItem can be requested
    Due (date)*Item can be requested

  • Select institution.

  • Follow screen prompts.

  • Check your patron record for status of request.

Loan Period

Most items are checked out for 21 days. Items may be renewed once online for 21 additional days if not needed elsewhere. For overdue items, additional renewals, or other problems, contact your own library.

Check Out Limits

Each library has its own limits regarding total items checked out, so you should inquire at your own library.

Searching

Quick Search

The Quick Search box is a powerful tool for users of the WILO Library Catalog. With it, you may quickly launch almost any kind of search, of any part of the WILO Catalog. Press the arrow at the end of the "Search by" box to see the types of searches you can perform. If you are not familiar with the Catalog, we suggest you look below the Quick Search box and choose the link for an individual type of search (e.g. author, title, etc.). For each type of search, you will find search advice and examples to help you with your research.

[back to top]

Too Many Results

Here are some ways that you can decrease the number of items you retrieve:

  1. Do a Subject search instead of a keyword search. LC Subject searches cover a standardized list of terms and do not search other fields, such as author, note, etc. Children's subject searches also can be used to search for specialized materials.

  2. Do a Title search instead of a keyword search. Sometimes you can find a handful of books with titles that begin with your topic, e.g. Management. As an alternative, you can type t: in front of a word to search for that word in the title of a work. This should also produce fewer results than a keyword search.

    t:France (finds materials with the word France anywhere in the title)

  3. Use the word AND to do a keyword search on more than one concept. The computer will look for items that mention both terms.

    television and children

  4. Use the word NOT to omit certain keywords. The computer will look for items that mention the first term but omit any items that mention the second term after the NOT.

    South America Not Brazil

  5. When viewing a record for an item you retrieved, check the "Subjects" field to find more specific headings/terms. Scroll down through the record for an item you retrieved until you see the Subject area. Here you will find suggestions for other subject terms you may use. A search on training, for example, will yield books on the narrower subjects given below:

    Employees--Training of
    Teams in the workplace


  6. Use the Limit/Sort button to narrow to a particular publication year or years, material type, language, etc. For example, you can have the computer rerun a search on World War II but limit the results to videotapes on the topic.

    Note: see Section on Limiting and Sorting for additional information.

Too Few Results

Here are some ways you can increase the number of items retrieved:

  1. Do a Keyword search instead of a LC Subject or Children's Subject search. A keyword search covers more fields, such as title, notes, etc.

  2. Use the word OR to search on variations of a concept. To give the computer more choices, use OR to link two words with the same meaning or two words, either of which would satisfy your needs.

    Amazon or Amazon.com
    Greek or Latin

  3. Use an asterisk * to truncate endings of words. Have the computer search for variations of a word by adding an asterisk. For example, to search for either training or trainers or train, type:

    train*

No Results

Here are some suggestions if your search gets zero results:

  1. Check your spelling. Unfortunately, the computer won't do this for you. You can use an asterisk * if you know at least part of a word.

    Dostoevsk* (will find Dostoevsky or Dostoevskii)

    If you want the computer to fill in more than 5 letters, use two asterisks:

    totalit** (for a search on totalitarianism)

  2. Do a keyword search. This is the most general type of search you can do.

  3. Make sure you are doing the correct type of search. Trying to search for an author in the journal name field usually won't work. Check that you have selected the correct type of search.

  4. Do a simple search on fewer keywords. Try simplifying your search by limiting the number of words you are stringing together with the word AND. For example, instead of personnel and training and computer-assisted instruction and evaluation, try the following:

    computer-assisted instruction and evaluation
    training and computer-assisted instruction

  5. Try the search in the entire MOBIUS catalog to see if another library/cluster has the materials you want. It's possible that your library just doesn't own the materials you need. Follow the link at the top of the screen to search the MOBIUS catalog or a catalog of another MOBIUS library or cluster.

Limiting

Limiting is done after a search is performed by selecting the Limit/Sort Search button. Only when using an Advanced Keyword search is the limiting done from the search screen. The number of records in a search can be reduced by limiting the search in one or more of the following ways:

  • Year of Publication: Specific year or range of years

    After 2000

After 1995 and Before 2003


  • Material Type

    BOOK/JOURNALSingle or multi volume printed works, or periodicals such as magazines, professional journals, and newspapers
    VIDEO/FILMVideotapes, or 8 or 16 mm film
    SPOKEN AUDIOLectures or addresses on tape or disk
    MUSIC AUDIORecorded music on tape or disk
    SOFTWAREComputer programs or computer readable data
    MUSIC SCOREPrinted music
    MAP/ATLASSingle or multi sheet maps, or bound volumes of maps
    ARCHIVAL MATLMaterials housed in a special collection
    PICTUREVisible image displayed on a flat surface
    KITCollection of several formats
    MIXED MATERIALCollection of several formats
    3-D OBJECTAn artifact such as a toy or a model of a person
    MS MUSICMusic produced by hand, either handwritten, typewritten or on a personal computer
    MS MAPMaps produced by hand, either handwritten, typewritten or on a personal computer
    MS PRINTText produced by hand, either handwritten, typewritten, or on a personal computer
    MICROFORMMicrofilm or microfiche

  • Language

    French

  • Publisher

    McGraw-Hill

    Note: a serial is a publication issued in successive parts such as a magazine, journal or newspaper.

Sorting

The default sort is by date. When using the Advanced Keyword search, records may be sorted by the following criteria:

  • Publication date
  • Alphabetical order by titlekey
  • Relevance to entered search

Relevance ranking is as follows:

Most relevantWord is one of the first eight words indexed for the same record
Next most relevantWord is one of the first thirty-two words indexed for the same record
Least relevantWord is indexed more than once in the same record

Making Lists/Printing Records
  • Search for records.

  • Mark each record. Place a check mark in the box called "Mark" beside each item you want.

  • Save marked records. Press the Save Marked Records button at the bottom of the page. You must save at each page before you continue or you will lose your marked records.

  • Retrieve your records. Select the Export Saved List button on the button bar.

  • Select appropriate options. You may email or save the list. You may also display it on your screen and print it out.

    Note: Remember to select the Clear Saved Records button to remove your marked records.

Displaying Records

The display formats are:

Brief displayDisplay includes title/author, medium and date. This is the default for all searches.
Extended displayDisplay includes title/author, location, circulation status, medium, and date. Select Extended Display button to view.
Full recordDisplay includes all information about a title including call number, location, and circulation status.
MARC displayMachine Readable Cataloging is a display useful mainly for library staff. Select MARC Display button to view.

Status

Some common WILO statuses are:

AvailableItem can be found on the shelf at the location given and can be checked out.
Due (date)Item is checked out and due on the date indicated.
Lib Use OnlyItem can be found on the shelf at the location given and cannot be checked out, but can be viewed at the library.
MissingItem cannot be found on the shelf and cannot be checked out or viewed.
